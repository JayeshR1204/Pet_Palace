Birman cat

View Gallary
  • Origin: Myanmar
  • Height: 8 to 10 inches
  • Weight: 4 - 7kgs
  • Color: Lilac, Red, Cream, Chocolate
  • Life-Span: 9-13 yrs
  • Personality: incredibly charming, gentle, loyal
  • Scientific Name: Sacred Birman


About This Pet

The Birman cat, also known as the "Sacred Cat of Burma," is a breed of domestic cat that originated in Burma (now Myanmar). Birmans are medium to large-sized cats with a muscular build and a semi-longhaired coat. They have a distinctive color-point pattern, similar to Siamese cats, with a lighter body color and darker points on their ears, face, paws, and tail. Their eyes are usually blue and expressive. The Birman cat has a silky, medium-length coat that is typically low-shedding and requires moderate grooming. Their fur is usually light in color, such as cream or fawn, with the darker points contrasting against the body.


-:    Personality    :-

Birmans are known for their gentle and affectionate nature. They are generally sociable, friendly, and enjoy human companionship. Birmans are often described as being people-oriented and tend to form strong bonds with their owners. They are often calm and adaptable, making them suitable for various living situations. Birmans are generally sociable cats that enjoy the company of both humans and other pets. They are not typically solitary animals and prefer being part of a family environment. Birmans will often greet guests and are known to get along well with children and other pets when properly introduced.Birmans are considered intelligent cats. They can learn and adapt quickly, making them trainable and open to interactive play. They enjoy puzzle toys, games, and other mentally stimulating activities.


-:    Grooming    :-

Regular brushing helps keep the Birman's coat in good condition and prevents matting. Using a soft-bristle brush or a comb with wide teeth, gently brush their fur at least once or twice a week. This helps remove loose hair, prevents tangles, and promotes a healthy coat. Birmans have a dense undercoat that may require extra attention during shedding seasons. Using a comb or a grooming tool specifically designed for undercoat removal can help remove loose hair and prevent matting.Birmans generally do not require frequent baths unless they get particularly dirty. However, occasional bathing can help keep their coat clean and fresh. Regular nail trims are important for any cat, including Birmans. Trim their nails every few weeks using a proper cat nail trimmer.


-:    Care & Maintenance    :-

Always ensure that your Birman has access to clean and fresh water. Regularly check and refill their water bowl to keep them hydrated. Provide your Birman cat with a balanced and nutritious diet. Choose high-quality cat food that meets their specific life stage (kitten, adult, or senior). Consult your veterinarian for recommendations on the appropriate portion sizes and feeding schedule. Provide a clean litter box for your Birman cat. Scoop the litter box daily to remove waste, and completely change the litter and clean the box on a regular basis. Birmans typically prefer fine-grain litter and may appreciate having multiple litter boxes in different areas of the house.Birmans are intelligent cats and benefit from mental and physical stimulation. Provide them with toys, scratching posts, and interactive play sessions to keep them engaged and entertained.


-:    History    :-

The exact history of the Birman cat is shrouded in legends and mystery. According to one popular legend, Birmans were believed to be sacred temple cats that resided in Buddhist temples in Burma (now Myanmar) centuries ago. The story goes that these cats were companions to the temple priests and were highly revered.One version of the legend tells the tale of Mun-Ha, a temple priest, and his beloved cat Sinh. During a raid on the temple, Mun-Ha was attacked and killed. While these legends add an air of mysticism to the breed's history, the true origin of the Birman cat is somewhat unclear. The breed was first recognized in France in the early 20th century.The breed gained recognition and popularity in France and eventually spread to other countries. The first Birmans were exhibited in cat shows in the 1920s. Over time, selective breeding and efforts to maintain the breed's distinct characteristics led to the establishment of the modern Birman breed standards.
