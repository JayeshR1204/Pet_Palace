British Shorthair

View Gallary
  • Origin: Great Britain
  • Height: 12 to 14 inches
  • Weight: 4-7 kgs
  • Color: black ,ebony, blue ,gray, chocolate
  • Life-Span: 18 to 20 years
  • Personality: playful, affectionate cat
  • Scientific Name: Ace, Blimey


About This Pet

British Shorthairs are medium to large-sized cats with a solid and muscular body. The British Shorthair's coat is dense and plush, with a crisp texture. They come in various colors and patterns, including solid colors like blue, black, white, cream, red, and chocolate, as well as tabby, tortoiseshell, and colorpoint patterns. British Shorthairs are known for their calm and reserved nature. They tend to be independent but also enjoy the company of their human family members.British Shorthairs are generally a healthy breed with few specific breed-related health issues.


-:    Personality    :-

British Shorthairs are generally independent cats who enjoy their alone time.These cats are typically reserved and observant. They may take some time to warm up to new people or situations. They prefer to assess their environment and observe from a distance before fully engaging.They can be self-sufficient and are often fine with being left alone for longer periods compared to some other breeds.This reserved nature adds to their dignified and calm demeanor.British Shorthairs are known for their gentle and easygoing nature. They are generally good-natured and not prone to aggressive behavior.


-:    Grooming    :-

The British Shorthair has a dense and plush coat that requires regular maintenance.British Shorthairs typically do not require frequent bathing unless they get dirty or have specific skin conditions. It's essential to follow proper bathing techniques and ensure the cat feels secure and comfortable throughout the process.As with any cat breed, regular eye and ear care is important. Check your British Shorthair's eyes for any discharge or signs of irritation.Like all cats, British Shorthairs' nails should be trimmed regularly to prevent them from becoming too long or sharp.In addition to regular grooming, it's crucial to schedule regular veterinary check-ups for your British Shorthair.


-:    Care & Maintenance    :-

Provide your British Shorthair with a balanced and nutritious diet that is appropriate for their age, weight, and health condition.Provide a clean and easily accessible litter box for your British Shorthair. Scoop the litter box daily to remove waste and clumps, and replace the litter regularly.While British Shorthairs tend to be less active compared to some other breeds, it's important to provide them with regular exercise and playtime.British Shorthairs enjoy a stimulating environment. Provide scratching posts, cat trees, and toys to keep them engaged.Schedule routine check-ups with a veterinarian to monitor your British Shorthair's health and address any concerns.


-:    History    :-

The British Shorthair's ancestors were domestic cats that arrived in Britain with the Romans around 2,000 years ago. In the late 19th century, cat shows gained popularity in Britain, leading to an increased interest in cat breeding and the development of specific breeds.To improve the breed and introduce new characteristics, breeders crossed British Shorthairs with Persian cats.In the early 20th century, the British Shorthair breed started to gain recognition and was exhibited in cat shows.The breed faced a setback during World War I and World War II when breeding programs were disrupted, and many cats were lost due to the war.
