Egyptian Mau

View Gallary
  • Origin: Egypt
  • Height: 7 to 11 inches
  • Weight: 3 - 5 kg
  • Color: silver, bronze or smoke
  • Life-Span: 9 to 13 years
  • Personality: Affectionate and friendly with everyone
  • Scientific Name:Felis catus


About This Pet

Egyptian Mau striking features include a sleek and muscular body, a medium-sized frame, and an exquisite spotted coat that comes in silver, bronze, or smoke colors. These cats thoroughly enjoy interactive play and mental stimulation, and their mischievous and playful nature adds charm to their character. While they may be cautious around strangers, they form strong bonds with their human companions and exhibit loyalty and affection. Egyptian Maus adapt well to different environments and can coexist with other pets. Their short to medium-length coat requires minimal grooming, making them relatively low-maintenance in terms of grooming needs. However, they do thrive in environments that provide ample opportunities for physical activity and mental engagement. Regular veterinary care and a balanced diet are essential to ensure their overall well-being. Egyptian Maus make for exceptional companions, captivating their owners with their grace, intelligence, and ancient charm.


-:    Personality    :-

Egyptian Maus are generally friendly cats that enjoy the company of their human companions. They are known to be affectionate and form strong bonds with their family members. Egyptian Maus are highly intelligent cats that display a curious nature. They are known for their problem-solving abilities and enjoy exploring their surroundings. Egyptian Maus have an active and playful nature. They love engaging in interactive play sessions and enjoy toys that stimulate their hunting instincts. Egyptian Maus have a keen sense of observation. They are alert and watchful, often keeping a close eye on their surroundings. Egyptian Maus are known for their vocalizations. They may use a range of sounds to communicate their needs, desires, or to get attention from their owners.


-:    Grooming    :-

The Egyptian Mau has a short to medium-length coat that requires minimal grooming compared to some other cat breeds. Egyptian Maus have a dense and silky coat that doesn't mat easily. Regular brushing once or twice a week is usually sufficient to keep their coat in good condition. Egyptian Maus are generally clean cats that groom themselves efficiently. They usually do not require frequent baths unless they get exceptionally dirty. Regular nail trimming is important for all cats, including Egyptian Maus. dental hygiene is important for Egyptian Maus. Egyptian Maus are generally healthy cats, but occasional eye discharge may occur. grooming, providing a balanced diet, regular veterinary check-ups, and parasite prevention are crucial for maintaining the overall health and well-being of your Egyptian Mau.


-:    Care & Maintenance    :-

Provide your Egyptian Mau with a high-quality cat food that is appropriate for their age, health condition, and activity level. Egyptian Maus are active and intelligent cats that thrive with mental and physical stimulation. Provide them with interactive toys, scratching posts, puzzle feeders, and vertical climbing spaces to keep them engaged and prevent boredom. Rotate and introduce new toys regularly to keep their interest. Egyptian Maus have a playful nature and enjoy interactive play sessions. Egyptian Maus are sociable cats that enjoy human interaction. Spend quality time with your cat, offering play, affection, and gentle grooming. Egyptian Mau is an individual with unique needs and preferences. Observe and understand your cat's behavior, and adapt your care routine accordingly.


-:    History    :-

Some historians and researchers believe that the Egyptian Mau is a direct descendant of the spotted cats that were once revered in ancient Egypt. They suggest that these cats lived alongside humans, providing companionship and serving as guardians of homes and food stores, keeping them free from pests. Over the centuries, as Egypt's influence spread and trade routes connected with other regions, cats might have been carried to other parts of the world, contributing to the development of various cat breeds. While the exact origins of the Egyptian Mau are not fully documented, various historical and archaeological evidence points to its ancient lineage. In modern times, the Egyptian Mau's story took a more documented turn. In the 1950s, a Russian princess named Natalie Troubetskoy brought a spotted cat from Italy to the United States, and this cat, named "Baba," became the foundation of the Egyptian Mau breed in the Western world.
