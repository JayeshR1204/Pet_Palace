Japanese Bobtail

View Gallary
  • Origin: Japan
  • Height: 8 to 9 inches
  • Weight: 3 - 4kgs.
  • Color: black / ebony, chocolate/ brown/ lavender/ silver, white
  • Life-Span: 15 to 18 years
  • Personality: active, sweet, loving and highly intelligent
  • Scientific Name:maneki-neko


About This Pet

The Japanese Bobtail is a breed of domestic cat known for its distinctive short tail. The Japanese Bobtail is a medium-sized cat with a well-muscled body and a long, lean build. Its most notable feature is its short tail, which is usually about 3 to 4 inches long. The tail is often flexible and expressive, and it may have a unique curved or kinked shape. Apart from the tail, the breed has a wedge-shaped head, oval-shaped eyes, and large, upright ears. they may be prone to common feline health problems such as dental issues, obesity, and certain infections. Regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, and proper grooming can help maintain their overall health.


-:    Personality    :-

The Japanese Bobtail breed is known for its distinct personality traits. Japanese Bobtails are highly energetic cats that love to play. They enjoy interactive toys, puzzles, and games that challenge their intelligence. Their playful nature often persists into adulthood, making them an entertaining and engaging companion. Japanese Bobtails are known for their intelligence and curiosity. They are quick learners and enjoy mental stimulation. Japanese Bobtails form strong bonds with their human family members. They are known for their affectionate and loving nature, often seeking attention and physical contact. They enjoy being near their loved ones and may follow them around the house.


-:    Grooming    :-

Japanese Bobtails are meticulous self-groomers. They have a dense and silky coat that requires regular attention. They spend a significant amount of time licking themselves to keep their fur clean and tangle-free. This self-grooming helps to distribute natural oils throughout their coat, keeping it healthy and shiny. While Japanese Bobtails groom themselves, regular brushing helps remove loose hair, prevents matting, and reduces the amount of hair they may ingest during self-grooming. Good oral hygiene is crucial for maintaining the overall health of your Japanese Bobtail. Brushing their teeth regularly using a cat-specific toothbrush and toothpaste is recommended.


-:    Care & Maintenance    :-

Provide your Japanese Bobtail with a balanced and nutritious diet. High-quality commercial cat food formulated for their age, activity level, and any specific dietary needs should be the primary component of their diet. Consult with a veterinarian to determine the appropriate feeding schedule and portion sizes for your cat. Fresh and clean water should be available to your Japanese Bobtail at all times. Ensure that their water bowl is regularly cleaned and refilled. Japanese Bobtails are active and intelligent cats that require mental and physical stimulation. Provide them with plenty of toys, scratching posts, and interactive play sessions to keep them engaged and entertained. Additionally, create vertical spaces such as cat trees or shelves for them to climb and explore.


-:    History    :-

The Japanese Bobtail is believed to have originated in Japan over a thousand years ago. It is depicted in traditional Japanese art, including woodblock prints and ceramics, dating back to the 7th century. The Japanese Bobtail is closely associated with the iconic Japanese cat figurine called Maneki-neko or the "beckoning cat." Maneki-neko is often depicted as a Japanese Bobtail with one paw raised in a beckoning gesture, believed to bring good fortune and luck to its owner. The Japanese Bobtail has had significant cultural and religious importance in Japan. It was considered a symbol of good luck and prosperity and was believed to ward off evil spirits. The cat's distinctive bobbed tail, called "pom-pom" in Japanese, was highly regarded and considered a desirable trait.
