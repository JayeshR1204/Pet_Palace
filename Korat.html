Korat

View Gallary
  • Origin: Phimai, Thailand
  • Height: 11 to 13 inches
  • Weight: 3-5 kgs
  • Color: blue with silver-tipped fur
  • Life-Span: 13 to 15 years
  • Personality: mild temperament
  • Scientific Name: Si sawat, Malet


About This Pet

The Korat has a distinctive appearance. It is a medium-sized cat with a muscular body and a semi-cobby build.The coat is short, smooth, and silver-tipped, giving it a shimmering appearance.The Korat's coat is predominantly a solid, bluish-gray color, known as "korat blue. Korats are known for their affectionate and gentle nature. They are highly devoted to their human companions and often form strong bonds with their owners.Korats are generally peaceful and enjoy a calm and stable environment. They are not typically aggressive or overly demanding.Korats are generally healthy cats with no breed-specific health issues. However, responsible breeders should regularly test their breeding cats for common feline health conditions to ensure the overall health and well-being of the breed.


-:    Personality    :-

Korats are known to be highly affectionate and deeply devoted to their human companions.They form strong bonds with their owners and enjoy spending time with them. They often seek physical contact and love to cuddle and be close to their humans.They are not known for being overly active or hyperactive.Korats are intelligent cats that enjoy mental stimulation. They are quick learners and can be trained to perform tricks or respond to commands. They also enjoy interactive toys and puzzle games that challenge their minds. While Korats have a calm nature, they still have a playful side. They enjoy interactive play sessions with their owners and may engage in games of chase, batting at toys, or climbing.


-:    Grooming    :-

The Korat's coat is naturally low-maintenance and does not typically require extensive brushing. Korat cats are generally clean animals and do not require frequent bathing.It's important to note that excessive bathing can strip the coat of its natural oils, so it should be done sparingly. Regular nail trimming is essential for all cats, including Korats. Trim your cat's nails every few weeks using cat-specific nail clippers or a grinder. Good oral hygiene is important for your cat's overall health. Establish a regular dental care routine for your Korat, including brushing their teeth with a cat-friendly toothbrush and toothpaste. Check your Korat's ears and eyes regularly for any signs of infection, discharge, or irritation.


-:    Care & Maintenance    :-

Provide a balanced and nutritious diet to meet your Korat's dietary needs. Choose high-quality cat food that is appropriate for their age, considering whether they are a kitten, adult, or senior.Always provide fresh and clean water for your Korat cat. Regularly refill their water bowl and ensure it is easily accessible to them.Schedule regular check-ups with a veterinarian to monitor your Korat's health and address any potential issues. Korats are moderately active cats that benefit from regular exercise and mental stimulation. As mentioned earlier, the grooming needs of a Korat are minimal. Regular brushing to remove loose hair and occasional baths as needed are usually sufficient.


-:    History    :-

The Korat is mentioned in ancient Thai literature and artwork, which indicates its long-standing presence in the region. It is believed that the breed originated in the northeastern part of Thailand, specifically in the province of Nakhon Ratchasima, which was formerly known as Korat.The Korat has been highly regarded in Thai culture and has been associated with good luck and fortune. Historically, the Korat cat was primarily kept by Thai nobility and was considered a valuable and treasured companion.The breed gained international recognition in the mid-20th century when a pair of Korats was imported to the United States. Jean L. Johnson, an American cat enthusiast, obtained the cats and began breeding them. Today, Korats can be found in various countries, and they continue to be cherished for their beauty, affectionate nature, and historical significance.
