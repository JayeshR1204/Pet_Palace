Scottish Fold

View Gallary
  • Origin: Scotland
  • Height: 8 to 10 inches
  • Weight: 4-6 kgs
  • Color: white, black, blue, red, and cream
  • Life-Span: 13 to 15 years
  • Personality: very even temperament,Active
  • Scientific Name: lop-eared or lops


About This Pet

Scottish Folds are medium-sized cats with a well-rounded body and a sweet expression.They have large, round eyes that contribute to their cute and endearing look.Scottish Folds have a dense, medium-length coat that can come in various colors and patterns.Patterns can range from solid colors to tabby, tortoiseshell, and colorpoint patterns. Scottish Folds are known for their friendly and affectionate nature. Scottish Folds are generally gentle and adaptable cats, making them suitable for various living situations.These cats are known for being intelligent and curious. They enjoy exploring their surroundings and playing with toys.


-:    Personality    :-

Scottish Folds are generally very affectionate cats. They enjoy being close to their human companions and will often seek out cuddles and petting. Scottish Folds are not overly active or hyperactive cats.These cats are known for their intelligence and curiosity. They enjoy exploring their surroundings and may be interested in investigating new objects or areas in the home. Scottish Folds are typically sociable and get along well with other pets and children.They are known for being friendly and approachable, making them a great choice for families.While Scottish Folds are expressive through their body language, they are not particularly vocal cats.


-:    Grooming    :-

Regular brushing helps to keep the coat of a Scottish Fold clean and free of mats. Scottish Folds generally do not require frequent bathing unless they get particularly dirty or have a skin condition that requires it. Pay special attention to the folded ears of Scottish Folds, as they can be prone to wax build-up and infections. -:    Care & Maintenance    :-

Provide your Scottish Fold with a balanced and nutritious diet that meets their specific needs. Consult with a veterinarian to determine the best diet plan for your cat.Provide a clean litter box that is easily accessible to your Scottish Fold. Scoop the litter box daily to remove waste and maintain cleanliness.Vaccinations, parasite control, dental care, and overall physical examinations are important parts of their healthcare routine. Engage your Scottish Fold in regular play sessions to keep them physically and mentally active.Regular grooming is essential to keep your Scottish Fold's coat in good condition.


-:    History    :-

The history of Scottish Fold cats begins in Scotland in the early 1960s. The breed gained recognition and popularity over time. In 1966, the Scottish Fold was officially recognized by the Governing Council of the Cat Fancy (GCCF) in the United Kingdom.To ensure the welfare of the breed, responsible breeding practices were established to avoid breeding two cats with folded ears together.The Scottish Fold gained international recognition and acceptance by various cat associations, including The International Cat Association (TICA) and the Cat Fanciers' Association (CFA) in the United States.It's important to note that while the folded ears are the defining characteristic of the Scottish Fold breed, not all Scottish Folds have folded ears.Schedule regular check-ups with a veterinarian to ensure your Scottish Fold's health and well-being.
