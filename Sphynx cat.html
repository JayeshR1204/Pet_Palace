Sphynx cat

View Gallary
  • Origin: Toronto, Canada
  • Height: 8 to 10 inches
  • Weight: 4-7 kgs
  • Color: white, black, red, brown, lavender
  • Life-Span: 12 to 14 years
  • Personality: sociable, playful, and entertaining pets
  • Scientific Name: Canadian Sphynx


About This Pet

Sphynx cats are easily recognized by their nearly hairless bodies. They have a soft, warm-to-the-touch skin that feels similar to a peach or a suede.While Sphynx cats are often described as being "hairless," they do actually have a fine layer of fuzz that covers their bodies.Despite their lack of fur, Sphynx cats require regular grooming. Their skin produces oil, which needs to be wiped off with a soft cloth or pet wipe to prevent it from building up.Sphynx cats are known for their affectionate and social nature. They are often described as being "people-oriented" and enjoy being the center of attention.


-:    Personality    :-

Sphynx cats are known for their love and need for physical contact. They enjoy being close to their human companions and often seek out warmth and comfort by snuggling up on laps, shoulders, or under blankets.They are known to be "velcro cats" that will stick to their owners like glue.Sphynx cats thrive on attention and interaction. They are highly sociable and enjoy being the center of attention.Sphynx cats are known for their playful nature. They have a natural curiosity and love to explore their surroundings. Sphynx cats are smart and quick learners. They can be taught tricks, respond to their names, and even learn to walk on a leash.


-:    Grooming    :-

Sphynx cats have a unique skin type that requires attention. Their skin produces natural oils, which can accumulate and lead to greasiness if not properly managed. Sphynx cats need periodic baths to keep their skin clean and free from oil buildup. Use a gentle cat-specific shampoo recommended by your veterinarian.Sphynx cats have large ears that may accumulate wax and debris. Regular ear cleaning is necessary to prevent infections and maintain good ear health.Like all cats, Sphynx cats need their nails trimmed regularly. Use cat-specific nail clippers or a nail grinder designed for pets.Good oral hygiene is important for Sphynx cats. Regular brushing with a cat-specific toothbrush and toothpaste helps prevent dental issues such as gum disease and tooth decay.


-:    Care & Maintenance    :-

Sphynx cats require regular grooming to maintain healthy skin. This includes wiping their skin with a soft cloth or pet wipe to remove excess oils and debris. Sphynx cats are more sensitive to temperature changes, both hot and cold. Ensure that your home is kept at a comfortable temperature range to prevent overheating or chilling. Sphynx cats' skin can be prone to dryness. Applying a gentle and hypoallergenic moisturizer, specifically formulated for cats, can help keep their skin hydrated.Feed your Sphynx cat a balanced and high-quality cat food. Consult your veterinarian to determine the appropriate diet based on their age, weight, and any specific dietary needs.


-:    History    :-

The history of the Sphynx cat is relatively recent compared to many other cat breeds. The origin of the Sphynx cat can be traced back to the 1960s in Toronto, Canada.Prune was mated with other cats, and over time, a small breeding program for hairless cats began. The first Sphynx cat was exported to the United States from Canada. This marked the introduction of the breed to a wider audience. The Sphynx cat breed gained recognition from cat associations, including The International Cat Association (TICA) and the Cat Fanciers' Association (CFA). The Sphynx cat has gained popularity worldwide and is recognized by major cat associations. The breed continues to be bred and developed while maintaining the hairless trait and adhering to breed standards.
