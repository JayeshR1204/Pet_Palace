Toyger

View Gallary
  • Origin: United States
  • Height: 10 to 13 inches
  • Weight: 5-7 kgs
  • Color: brown, reddish brown or deep orange
  • Life-Span: 11 to 13 years
  • Personality: friendly, outgoing temperament
  • Scientific Name: purebred


About This Pet

Toygers are medium to large-sized cats with a muscular build. Their coat has distinctive tiger-like markings, with dark stripes on an orange or brown background. Toygers are generally friendly, social, and outgoing cats. They tend to be affectionate and enjoy human companionship. They are known for their playful nature and often get along well with other pets.Toygers are considered to be intelligent cats. They are curious by nature and enjoy interactive toys and games that provide mental stimulation.They can be trained to perform tricks and are known for their ability to learn quickly.


-:    Personality    :-

Toygers are generally social cats that enjoy being around people and other pets. They are known for their friendly and affectionate nature.Toygers have a playful and active nature. They enjoy interactive playtime, puzzle toys, and games that provide mental and physical stimulation.Toygers are considered intelligent cats. They are quick learners and can pick up tricks and commands with relative ease.Toygers are naturally curious cats. They like to explore their surroundings and investigate new things.Despite their wild-looking appearance, Toygers tend to have a gentle temperament. They are generally good with children and can adapt well to households with other pets, including dogs.


-:    Grooming    :-

Toygers have short, dense coats that are easy to maintain. They do not typically require extensive brushing or regular grooming sessions.Toygers generally do not require frequent baths unless they get particularly dirty or have a skin condition. Regular nail trimming is essential for all cats, including Toygers. Trimming their nails every few weeks can help prevent them from becoming too long or sharp.Maintaining good dental hygiene is crucial for cats. Brushing your Toyger's teeth regularly with a cat-friendly toothpaste and toothbrush can help prevent dental issues such as tartar buildup and gum disease. oygers' ears should be checked regularly for any signs of dirt, wax buildup, or redness.


-:    Care & Maintenance    :-

Provide a balanced and nutritious diet that is appropriate for your Toyger's age, weight, and activity level. High-quality commercial cat food that is formulated for active breeds is usually a good choice. Toygers are active cats that require regular exercise and mental stimulation. Provide interactive toys, scratching posts, and climbing structures to keep them physically and mentally engaged. Ensure that your Toyger has access to a clean litter box at all times. Scoop the litter box daily and change the litter regularly to maintain hygiene. Toygers generally prefer unscented clumping litter, but individual preferences may vary.Schedule regular veterinary check-ups for your Toyger to monitor their health, update vaccinations, and receive preventive treatments for parasites.


-:    History    :-

The Toyger cat breed was developed in the 1980s by Judy Sugden, a breeder in the United States.To create the Toyger breed, Sugden used domestic Bengal cats with distinctive coat patterns and physical traits, such as rosettes and a muscular body structure. Sugden focused on producing Toygers with well-defined vertical stripes, circular markings on the head, and a rich, vibrant coat color reminiscent of a tiger. The breeding program involved careful selection of parent cats that displayed desired traits, such as clear and bold markings, good body conformation, and a friendly temperament. In 2000, the Toyger breed gained recognition by The International Cat Association (TICA) as an experimental breed. This allowed Toygers to be shown in TICA cat shows, and their pedigrees could be recorded.Toygers continue to be bred by dedicated breeders who aim to maintain and improve the breed's distinctive appearance and friendly personality.
