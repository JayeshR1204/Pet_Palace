About This Pet

The Bombay cat is a breed of domestic cat known for its sleek black coat and copper or gold eyes. It is named after the city of Bombay (now Mumbai) in India because its coat color resembles the black leopards found in the region. Bombay cats are generally healthy and don't have any specific breed-related health issues. sleek coat of the Bombay cat is relatively easy to maintain. Regular brushing can help keep their coat clean and remove loose hair. They should also receive regular dental care, such as teeth brushing, to prevent dental issues. Providing them with a balanced diet and engaging them in regular play sessions can help keep them physically and mentally stimulated.

-:    History    :-

The American Curl is a relatively new and distinct breed of cat that originated in Lakewood, California, in the early 1980s. The breed's unique characteristic is its curled ears, which give it a distinctive and appealing appearance. The curled ears of Shulamith were not a result of selective breeding but rather a natural genetic mutation. This trait is caused by a dominant gene that affects the cartilage of the ear, causing it to curl backward. Recognizing the unique and desirable trait of Shulamith's curled ears, the Rugas decided to establish a breeding program to develop a new breed based on this characteristic. the American Curl gained popularity and attracted the interest of cat enthusiasts worldwide. Breeders in different countries contributed to the breed's development, helping establish a diverse and robust population.
